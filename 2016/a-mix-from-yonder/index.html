YouTube has come along some leaps and bounds since I neglected it for hosting mixes in favour of Mixcloud 2 years ago. Their new licensing terms  mean that a previous mix of mine which had a 7 minute silent gap in the middle due to an unresolvable copyright claim on the track LTJ Bukem - Music, now has the track faithfully restored, and the deal is that sometimes there might be adverts.

Considering Mixcloud loves the adverts whether you pay a subscription or not - I'd contest this is very reasonable. Additionally, if I ever work out a relatively simple way to achieve this, I can whack some video over the top too (not really up for splashing out on Serato Video DJ just yet, however, Serato need to sort out their live streaming capabilities first! ... Rather than releasing a 'big update' (v. 1.9.0) of their flagship product Serato DJ - which is just thinly veiled pompery for them pushing some new music subscription service that I shan't even mention the name of...) in my ever so humble opinion. anyhow.
